Algorithm Overview

All three algorithms share a single quantum image representation — NEQR — and run on Qiskit's statevector and QASM simulators, faithfully modelling future gate-based quantum hardware.

Figure 1. Side-by-side results for all three algorithms on a structured test image: original → inverted → similarity score → quantum edge map → classical Sobel baseline.

NEQR — Novel Enhanced Quantum Representation

Every image is encoded as a quantum superposition spanning all pixel positions simultaneously:

$$|I\rangle \;=\; \frac{1}{\sqrt{2^n}} \sum_{Y=0}^{2^{n_r}-1} \sum_{X=0}^{2^{n_c}-1} |f(Y,X)\rangle \otimes |Y\rangle \otimes |X\rangle \tag{1}$$

where $|f(Y,X)\rangle$ is the 8-qubit value register encoding grayscale intensity $\in [0, 255]$, and $|Y\rangle$, $|X\rangle$ are the $n_r$- and $n_c$-qubit position registers. Quantum parallelism means a single circuit evaluation simultaneously encodes all $2^{2n}$ pixels.
